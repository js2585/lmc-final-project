Introduction

Welcome to RealDealReveal, your educational resource in the fight against AI misinformation! RealDealReveal is your best resource for understanding the current uses of generative AI in journalism, ethical implications of AI images, and instructional tools to help you identify AI generated images.

Background

The emergence of AI image generation models came with a myriad of negative effects on the journalistic integrity. Misinformation has never been easy to spread online, a simple prompt to image generators such as Midjourney, DALL-E, and Stable Diffusion gives users instant access to anything they can imagine. Artificial images are frequently used to spread misinformation and political propaganda. Being able to tell the difference between real and fake images has never been harder - and never more important.


Not only does AI art affect journalism, but it also has a direct impact on artists and photographers. Economic opportunity loss, plagiarism, and copyright infringement are just a few of the harms that digital artists have mentioned because of generative AI models. There is little incentive for companies to commission artwork when cheap art is available instantly using generative models.


One of the main criticisms of generative AI is that many of the models used today have been trained on copywritten data, presenting an ethical and legal dilemma for those whose content has been stolen. Many artists feel that their ideas and artwork are being stolen by AI that have trained on their previous works. Due to the permanence of the internet, it is likely that even with legal action, it will be extremely difficult to dispose of these models. In the interest of digital artists, the best course of action is to mitigate the existing damages done the industry using digital tools and education. To normal users, this means increased awareness on the proliferation of these images and active learning to better identify misinformation before hitting post. Key Differences

Being able to identify the differences between AI generated images and real images is getting harder as models improve, but even just a few simple tips can greatly increase your chances of spotting AI. Here's a few that you should look for:


  1. Look at the small details: AI art isn't perfect and often these imperfections are in the details. For animals and humans look at the eyes, ears, fingers, and limbs to spot blurring, clipping, or irregularities that wouldn't appear on a normal picture.
  2. Lighting: AI can struggle to create realistic lighting patterns. Look at shadows and reflections to see if the angles align with their light sources.
  3. Smoothing: AI often struggles to create realistic textures, so we suggest looking at surfaces and skin to spot unnatural smoothing. If it looks too good to be true, it likely is!
  4. Text: AI often generates shaky or even illegible text. Look towards signs and scripts to see if the text is legible or even in a real language!
  5. Realism: Context is crucial to spotting fake images. If an image is out of place or seems impossible, think twice before believing it!
